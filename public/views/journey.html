OUR JOURNEY

The journey of DRISTI began when Vikas Pandey, a 4th year Mechanical Engineering student of the institute got together with like-minded individuals of his college and decided to do something about the problems plaguing the villages neighbouring the college, namely Panchauli and Masu. From its humble beginnings as ‘The BIT Mesra Boys’ Club’ back in the winters of 2013, DRISTI today consists of upwards of 600 members, who are all dedicated to the purpose of betterment of the villagers, and helping them prosper and learn from educated students of the college. Team DRISTI’s activities are overlooked by 4 major sub-groups: Diksha, Damini, Daksha and Disha, which serve the purposes of enlightening the villagers about govt. schemes, encouraging women empowerment in the villages, imparting basic computer skills, and providing career counselling to the villages’ various students respectively. Also, F.C. DRISTI and DRISTI Tuition Classes help the children of these villages to understand the importance of sports and education in their life, and encourage them to do well in both spheres of their life. At present DRISTI has three chapters in MESRA, ALLAHABAD and DEOGHAR. DRISTI has recently been registered as an NGO and this has boosted the morals of all the volunteers. DRISTI has been fundamental in the overall development of the aforementioned villages that we adopted, and Team DRISTI will continue its journey, and therefore, the fulfilment of its purposes in the future too.
