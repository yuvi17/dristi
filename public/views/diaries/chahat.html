CHAHAT SINGLA

Having completed about three years in DRISTI, I can honestly say becoming a part of DRISTI was the best thing I have done so far. It has always been a mutually benefitting relationship for me. I teach, and I learn in the process. I learn to be a better person, understand the inequality in society and work towards its removal.
DRISTI has made me grow as a person, not only inculcating qualities like self-confidence, creativity and teaching skills, but also honing my managerial abilities. Being a part of DRISTI and going to the village wasn’t only about teaching the kids their routine subjects but also exposing them towards various opportunities for growth available around them, yet little known. Even though they see hundreds of volunteers come and go, somehow it feels genuine when they hug me and politely ask, “Di, kal aaoge na (You will come back tomorrow, won’t you)?”
The best part about the whole experience is the love and respect that the kids give. They are always so excited to see us coming, and without fail greet us with beaming smiles. Working at DRISTI we also faced many challenges, but the constant support from seniors and a motivation to see those dazzling little smiles kept us going. DRISTI lives upto its name and does provide a vision for change. I am grateful to DRISTI for providing me a channel to foster change in the society.
