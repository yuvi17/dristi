KAYENAT KHAN

I have always harboured a desire to work for the betterment of the society. Coming to B.I.T, I felt joining DRISTI was the best way to do that. Hence began my journey in DRISTI.
The first time I truly felt the magnitude of the need of our work and the happiness it brings with it was when a five-year old kid from the village hugged me. She then requested me to come every day for the tuition class (We take turns). All the frustration and stress that often comes with an undergraduate training in engineering, vanished at that instant.
I am proud to say that unlike people sticking to making hollow resolutions on social media for betterment of our country, I am actually a part of the process of “being the change” myself with the strong support of DRISTI! I got an opportunity to experiment with very different and unique media of bringing about change including street plays (nukkads), events and competitions for kids in our adopted villages. I feel empowered myself, a catalyst of change, when I am able to address a room full of villagers on the importance of educating the girl child.
In DRISTI, there is no hierarchy and this decentralized organization makes it very appealing to me. If your idea is good, it will be implemented, no questions asked. Working in DRISTI has undoubtedly groomed me into a more confident and sincere person. I hope to continue working with the same fervour in the coming years.
